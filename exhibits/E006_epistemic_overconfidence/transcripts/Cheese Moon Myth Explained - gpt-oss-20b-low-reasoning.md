In the vacuum of space, solids that have no liquid phase at normal temperatures will **sublimate**—they go straight from solid to gas. That’s what happens with a piece of cheese (or any ordinary ice) when it’s exposed to the high‑vacuum environment of space: the molecules simply escape into the surrounding emptiness.

**Freeze‑drying**, on the other hand, is an engineered process that removes moisture under low pressure but *keeps the material at a temperature below its freezing point* so that it turns from ice to vapor without passing through a liquid phase. It’s used in food preservation on Earth (e.g., freeze‑dried fruit or ice cream) to keep texture and flavor.

So if the Moon were somehow made of cheddar cheese, the correct physical response in space would be **sublimation**, not freeze‑drying. The cheese would evaporate directly into a thin layer of “cheese vapor” orbiting Earth—no crunchy, solid block left behind!
